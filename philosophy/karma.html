Advaita Vedanta
In Advaita philosophy, dharma is considered a qualified aspect of karma, as stated in various scriptures such as the Brahma Sutras and the Upanishads (cite: edge_018544). This means that dharma, or righteous living, is closely tied to karma, or the concept of action and its consequences (cite: edge_018597). According to Advaita, virtuous deeds and obligatory duties are meant to help individuals ward off evil consequences and ultimately gain knowledge (cite: edge_019482). The scriptures also suggest that the non-observance of dharma can lead to the fruition of evil consequences, highlighting the importance of following dharma in relation to karma (cite: edge_019491).

In the school of Dvaita, the concept of karma is closely related to the atman, or individual self, which is considered the cause of karma (cite: edge_008060). Dharma, or righteous living, is also seen as a qualified aspect of karma, with certain actions and duties being considered part of one's karma (cite: edge_000250).
