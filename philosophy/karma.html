Advaita Vedanta
In Advaita philosophy, dharma is often seen as a qualified aspect of karma (cite: edge_018544). This means that dharma, or righteous living, is connected to karma, but they are not the same thing (cite: edge_018126). According to Advaita, dharma can be helpful in the rise of knowledge and is related to the stages of life (cite: edge_018604). Additionally, dharma is seen as a way to enjoy the fruits of our deeds, which is a part of karma (cite: edge_018991).
Dvaita Vedanta
In the school of Dvaita, the concept of karma is closely related to the atman, or individual self, which is considered the cause of karma (cite: edge_008060). The atman's actions and decisions lead to the accumulation of karma, which can result in various outcomes, including multiple births (cite: edge_007447). Dharma, or righteous living, is also connected to karma in Dvaita philosophy, and is sometimes considered a qualified aspect of karma (cite: edge_000250). However, dharma is also distinct from karma, and the two concepts are not always equivalent (cite: edge_007795).

Additionally, karma can have an impact on the atman, with the goal of ultimately undoing desires and achieving a higher state (cite: edge_006621).
