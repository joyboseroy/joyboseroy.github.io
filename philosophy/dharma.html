Advaita Vedanta
In Advaita philosophy, dharma is often associated with karma, as it is gathered from texts about lifelong performance (cite: edge_018599) and the duties pertaining to the stages of life (cite: edge_018604). Dharma is also seen as related to karma in terms of the enjoyment of the fruits of our deeds (cite: edge_018991) and the performance of obligatory duties to ward off evil consequences (cite: edge_019482). Additionally, dharma is sometimes connected to the concept of atman, as in the context of meditation (cite: edge_020055) and the importance of faith (cite: edge_019772). Overall, dharma is closely tied to the ideas of karma and atman in Advaita philosophy (cite: edge_018604).
Dvaita Vedanta
In the school of Dvaita, dharma is seen as a qualified aspect of karma, as it is related to the paths to the lordship of the objects of enjoyment (cite: edge_000250). Dharma is also considered a qualified aspect of brahma, with reference to the different results to which the gods are entitled (cite: edge_007650). Additionally, dharma is distinct from karma, as it involves doing what is permitted, rather than acting solely on one's own accord (cite: edge_007791). Dharma is also considered a cause of moksha, as even a small act of righteousness can become great and everlasting (cite: edge_007917).

Buddhism (Pali Canon)

It is also considered a qualified aspect of good deeds, as one should practice only good deeds (cite: edge_010103).
