Atman

Dharma

Moksha
In Buddhist philosophy, the concept of moksha is related to the idea of a "realized one" (cite: edge_008964). A realized one is said to both exist and not exist after death, which suggests that atman (the self) is distinct from moksha (cite: edge_008961). The Buddhist texts also state that a realized one "neither still exists nor no longer exists after death", further emphasizing the distinction between atman and moksha (cite: edge_008993). Additionally, the concept of dharma is considered a qualified aspect of moksha, implying that following certain principles or teachings can lead to liberation (cite: edge_008798).

Anatta
In Buddhist philosophy, the concept of anatta is distinct from the idea of atman, or a permanent self (cite: edge_008850). This means that the self is not seen as a fixed or unchanging entity (cite: edge_009103). According to Buddhist teachings, the self that once existed no longer survives, and is not permanent (cite: edge_008944). The idea of "I am" arises from grasping onto things, and consciousness is not considered to be the self (cite: edge_008423).
