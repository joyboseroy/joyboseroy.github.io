What each school says

Advaita Vedanta
In the school of Advaita, moksha is associated with the concept of dharma, which is considered a qualified aspect of moksha (cite: edge_016661). Dharma is also seen as a cause of moksha, leading to liberation (cite: edge_018605). Additionally, the concept of atman, or the self, is also linked to moksha, with atman being a qualified aspect of moksha (cite: edge_019919) and leading to freedom from attributes (cite: edge_020509). Ultimately, the pursuit of dharma and the understanding of atman are seen as essential for achieving moksha, or liberation (cite: edge_018992).
Dvaita Vedanta
In the school of Dvaita, the concept of moksha, or liberation, is closely tied to the atman, or soul (cite: edge_007004). The atman is seen as a cause of moksha, leading to release from the cycle of death and rebirth (cite: edge_000251). Dharma, or righteous living, is also considered a cause of moksha, leading to knowledge and ultimately liberation (cite: edge_007034). However, karma, or action, can both be a qualified aspect of moksha and obstruct it, depending on the context (cite: edge_020682, cite: edge_007914). Vishishtadvaita

The atman is also considered a qualified aspect of moksha, with the released soul being of the same nature as the devotee's self (cite: edge_006414). Additionally, dharma, or righteous living, is seen as a qualified aspect of moksha, with the pursuit of dharma leading to success and everlasting peace (cite: edge_001307). Ultimately, the atman, through true knowledge and freedom from passion, can lead to moksha, or the highest oneness (cite: edge_005536).

Buddhism (Pali Canon)
In Buddhist philosophy, the concept of moksha is related to the idea of a "realized one" (cite: edge_008964). A realized one is said to both exist and not exist after death, which suggests that atman (the self) is distinct from moksha (cite: edge_008961). The Buddhist texts also state that a realized one "neither still exists nor no longer exists after death", further emphasizing the distinction between atman and moksha (cite: edge_008993). Additionally, the concept of dharma is considered a qualified aspect of moksha, implying that following certain principles or teachings can lead to liberation (cite: edge_008798).
