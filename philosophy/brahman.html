Advaita Vedanta
In the school of Advaita, Brahman is considered to be identical to the atman, or the Self (cite: edge_021429). This idea is repeated throughout various Upanishads, with statements such as "the Self is verily Brahman" (cite: edge_021430). The Advaita school also suggests that dharma, or righteous living, is a qualified aspect of Brahman (cite: edge_020753). Ultimately, the goal of Advaita is to realize that the individual self is one with Brahman, and this understanding can lead to liberation (cite: edge_021404).

Ultimately, the Dvaita school seems to suggest that the atman and Brahman are related but distinct entities, with the atman being able to enjoy or experience Brahman, but not being identical to it (cite: edge_008036).
